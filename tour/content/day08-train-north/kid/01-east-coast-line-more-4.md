# The Warrior Queen Under the Platform
Here's a spooky secret about the station you just left. Some people say that a real warrior queen is buried underneath it. Her name was Boudicca, and about two thousand years ago she was one of the fiercest fighters in all of Britain. When the Romans invaded her land and treated her people cruelly, she raised a huge army, climbed onto her war chariot, and led them into battle. She and her warriors burned the Roman town of London right down to the ground. In the end the Romans beat her, but nobody has ever been quite sure what happened to her body.

And here's the legend. The story goes that Boudicca lies buried deep under King's Cross station, right below the platforms, maybe even under platform nine or ten. Now, is it true? Almost certainly not. Grown-up historians will tell you there's no real proof at all, and it's probably just a spooky old story. But think about where that spooky story lands. It means the fierce queen who battled the Romans is supposed to be sleeping just a few steps away from the famous magic wall, platform nine and three-quarters, where the trolley disappears into the bricks on the way to Hogwarts. A warrior queen and a wizard's train, tucked into the same corner of the same station. Not bad for a place most people just rush through.

The station itself is very old and very clever. It opened more than a hundred and seventy years ago, and when it was brand new it was the biggest railway station in the whole country. Look at a picture of the front sometime and you'll see two enormous curved arches of yellow brick, plain and strong, like two giant tunnels standing up on end.

And one more thing to spot, right at the very start of the journey. In the first few minutes after leaving, if you were looking out of the left-hand window, you might have seen a gigantic modern football stadium sliding by. That's the Emirates Stadium, the home of Arsenal Football Club, where sixty thousand fans roar every time somebody scores. So in just the first stretch of your trip, you passed a buried warrior queen, a magic wizard wall, and a giant football ground. And the train has barely got going.
