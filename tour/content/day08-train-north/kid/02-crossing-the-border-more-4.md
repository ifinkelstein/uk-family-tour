# The Silver Water and the Mountain You'll Climb
You're nearly there now, and the last little bit of the journey is full of things you're going to see up close very soon. So this is like a treasure map. Keep your eyes peeled.

First, watch out of the right-hand window for the sea to change its shape. Out there, the wide open ocean slowly turns into a huge shining arm of water that reaches in toward the land, like a giant silver river. That's called the Firth of Forth, and here's why it's exciting for you. In just two days, you're going to be out on that very water in a boat. You'll sail across it to a tiny island with a crumbling old stone abbey on it, called Inchcolm, where monks lived hundreds of years ago. And somewhere out on that water there's one of the most famous bridges in the whole world, a gigantic bridge made of red steel, built more than a hundred years ago, shaped like three enormous diamonds standing in a row. So the water you're gliding past right now is the water you'll be bobbing about on very soon. Give it a wave.

Now look ahead and a little to the left, because a mountain is about to rise up right in the middle of the city. A real one, green and steep with a bare rocky top. It's called Arthur's Seat, and it's an old, sleeping volcano. Don't worry, it hasn't erupted in more than three hundred million years, so it's perfectly safe. But here's the brilliant bit. In a few days, you are going to climb it. All the way up to the very top, with your own two feet, and from up there you'll be able to see the whole city, the sea, the bridges, everything, spread out below you like a map. So as it slides past the window, take a good long look and size it up. That's your mountain. You're going to conquer it.

Out on that abbey island, by the way, watch for something else when you visit. Puffins and seals and thousands of noisy seabirds live all around the Firth, wheeling over the water and squabbling on the rocks, and grey seals sometimes pop their heads up to stare at the passing boats like curious dogs. So the silver water you're gazing at now is going to be full of surprises when you finally get out on it.

And beyond the mountain, getting closer and closer, you'll spot another great lump of old volcano with a castle perched right on top. That's Edinburgh Castle, and it means you have very nearly arrived. Bags ready. Scotland is here.
