# How a King Had Fun
When Henry the Eighth was young, before he grew old and grumpy and enormous, he was one of the most sporty, energetic kings England ever had, and he built Hampton Court to be a giant playground for a king. Almost everywhere you walk today, Henry was once playing some kind of game.

His absolute favourite was jousting. Picture two knights in shining armour at opposite ends of a long sandy track, each on a galloping warhorse, each holding a lance as long as a lamppost, thundering straight at each other. The goal was to smash your lance against the other knight's shield and knock him clean off his horse, while the crowd roared. It was thrilling, it was showing off, and it was seriously dangerous. Henry did it right here, in an arena called the tiltyard, the very ground your Magic Garden now sits on.

When he was not jousting, Henry loved to hunt. He would ride out into the great park with his dogs and chase the deer, sometimes for a whole day at a time. He hunted birds too, using trained hawks and falcons that flew off his glove and dived on their prey, which is called hawking. He played bowls, rolling balls across the grass. He played that funny indoor tennis you heard about earlier, and he was brilliant at it. And when he wanted to sit still, he gambled, playing cards and dice for money, and he lost enormous amounts, because when you are the king, who is going to stop you?

He also feasted, and danced, and put on shows with music and costumes that went on late into the night. The truth is, young Henry treated being king a bit like being the star of an endless, glittering party, sport all day and celebration all night. It is easy to think of him only as the frightening king with all the wives. But for a while, he was the fittest, fastest, most fun-loving man at court, the one everybody wanted to watch. Keep that younger Henry in your mind, because next you will hear about the single terrible moment that may have turned the golden, sporty king into the scary one that history remembers.
