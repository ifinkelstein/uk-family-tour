# The World in One City
Get ready, because today you are going to do something almost magical. In just one day, in just one city, you are going to travel all the way around the world, and all the way back through time, and you will never even get on a plane.

Here is the secret. This morning you are walking into a building called the British Museum, and inside it are treasures from everywhere on Earth. Egyptian mummies. A giant stone king. Winged monsters as tall as a bus. Gold from a buried ship. It is as if somebody scooped up the most amazing things from thousands of years of history, from Egypt, from Iraq, from Greece, from all over the world, and piled them all under one enormous roof.

And here is the part grown ups sometimes forget to feel amazed about. You can just walk in. It is free. It has been free for almost three hundred years, ever since it first opened way back in 1759. Kings used to lock their treasures away where only rich, important people could see them. This museum was one of the very first anywhere to say, no, everybody can come in. Even you. Even someone visiting from another country. Anyone who is curious.

This afternoon you will visit a second treasure house, called the National Gallery, stuffed with some of the most famous paintings ever made, and that one is free too.

So think of today as a giant treasure hunt across the whole world. The whole planet, and thousands of years of history, all waiting inside a few big rooms. Ready? Let us go and find the treasure.
