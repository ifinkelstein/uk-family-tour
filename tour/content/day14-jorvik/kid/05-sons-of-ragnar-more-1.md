# Ragnar and the Snake Pit

Alright, gather round, because this is a proper Viking legend, full of drama. And I am going to be honest with you the whole way through, because a lot of this is a story the Vikings made up, not stuff that really happened. But what a story it is.

Once, so the tale goes, there was a mighty Viking king named Ragnar Lothbrok, and he raided far and wide across the seas, and everyone feared him. But one day his luck ran out. His ship was wrecked in a storm on the coast of northern England, and he was captured by an English king called Ælla. Now, King Ælla knew he had caught somebody famous, and he wanted to make sure everyone remembered it. So he had Ragnar thrown into a deep pit, and the pit was full of poisonous snakes.

And here is the moment that made the story famous forever. As Ragnar lay there among the hissing snakes, he was not afraid. He laughed. And he said a strange and spooky thing. He said, the little piglets would grunt if they knew how the old boar suffered. It was a riddle. The old boar was Ragnar himself. And the little piglets, well, those were his sons. He was warning King Ælla, with his last breath, that his boys were going to come for revenge, and they were going to be furious.

And they were. When Ivar the Boneless and his brothers heard how their father had died, the stories say they gathered the biggest Viking army anyone had ever seen and sailed for England, not to steal treasure and leave, but to conquer the whole place and hunt down King Ælla. And when they finally caught him, the legend says they gave him a truly horrible ending, so nasty that I am not even going to describe it. It has a scary name, the blood eagle, and that is all you are getting.

But now the honest part, because you deserve the truth. Did any of this really happen? Well, there really was a big Viking army, and it really did take York, and there really was a King Ælla who really did die fighting the Vikings near here. Those bits are true. But Ragnar himself might never have been one real person at all, the snake pit is almost certainly a made-up story, and most experts today think the horrible blood eagle was probably invented too, or came from someone misunderstanding an old Viking poem about eagles eating on a battlefield. So it is a brilliant, scary, exciting legend, wrapped around a few real facts, like a spooky story built on top of a real place. And the real place, where the real army really came, is right here, all around you.
