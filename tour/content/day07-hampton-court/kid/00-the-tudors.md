# The Family Behind This Whole Palace
Look around at this enormous palace. Every brick of it, every chimney, every golden ceiling was built by, or for, one single family. They were called the Tudors, and for more than a hundred years they were the kings and queens of England. There were only ever five of them, but two are famous all over the world, and you are about to spend the whole day inside their house.

The first is King Henry the Eighth. Picture a giant of a man, loud, clever and scary, with a red beard and a terrible temper, who loved feasting, tennis and hunting, and who got married six different times. Yes, six. Some of those wives ended very, very badly, and you will hear all about them today. Henry also did something almost no king had ever dared to do. He got into a gigantic argument with the most powerful religious leader in Europe, the Pope in Rome, and he won, and it changed his whole country forever.

The second famous Tudor is Henry's daughter, Queen Elizabeth the First. She became queen when she was twenty-five and ruled for forty-five years without ever getting married. Her time was so full of adventure, with sailing ships, explorers and brilliant new plays, that people still call it a golden age. A writer you may have heard of, William Shakespeare, wrote his plays for her London.

So as you walk around today, remember this. You are not just visiting an old building. You are stepping right into the home of one of the most powerful, dramatic and dangerous families in all of history. Keep your eyes open. They left their mark everywhere.
