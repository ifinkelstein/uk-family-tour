# The War of the Two Roses
So where did the Tudors come from? To find out, we have to go back to a real war with a surprisingly pretty name, the Wars of the Roses. Here is the wow part. It was a war inside one giant royal family, cousins against cousins, all fighting over who got to be king. One side had a red rose as its badge. The other side had a white rose. And for about thirty years they fought and fought, and lots of kings were crowned and then killed.

It all came down to one single morning. On a summer day in fourteen eighty-five, at a place called Bosworth, two armies smashed into each other. On one side was the king, Richard the Third. On the other was a young man named Henry Tudor. In the middle of the battle, King Richard was killed, and here is the amazing bit. The story says his golden crown rolled off his head and got stuck in a thornbush, and someone found it and placed it straight onto Henry's head, right there in the muddy field. Just like that, England had a new king, Henry the Seventh, the very first Tudor.

Then Henry did something clever. He married a princess from the other side, the white rose side, so the two families would stop fighting and become one. And to show everybody that the fighting was truly over, he invented a brand new badge, a red rose and a white rose squished together into a single flower. It is called the Tudor rose, and here is your mission for today. Look for it everywhere in this palace. It is carved into stone, painted on ceilings and set into windows. Every time you spot one, it is like finding a tiny secret message that says, this family united the country.

That first Henry's son grew up to be Henry the Eighth. And Henry the Eighth's children became the kings and queens who came next, all the way down to Elizabeth. One family, all in a row.
