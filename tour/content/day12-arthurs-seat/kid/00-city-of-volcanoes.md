# Sleeping Volcanoes Under a Whole City
Here is something wild about the city you are standing in. Almost the whole of Edinburgh is built on top of old volcanoes. Not one volcano, but a bunch of them. That giant rock the castle sits on? Volcano. The big green hill you are going to climb today? Volcano. Bumps and ridges all over the city? Volcanoes, or the leftover bits of them.

Now, do not worry. These volcanoes are extinct, which means dead, finished, done erupting forever. They last blew their tops around three hundred and forty million years ago. That is so long ago that there were no dinosaurs yet, none at all. Back then Scotland was not even here. It sat down near the equator where it is hot, and this spot was a steamy swamp beside the sea. Imagine that instead of chilly Edinburgh.

So where did the volcanoes go? Two things happened. First, over millions of years, the soft outside of each volcano wore away in the wind and rain, leaving only the super hard rock that had cooled deep inside. Then, much later, gigantic sheets of ice called glaciers came grinding over the whole country and scraped everything smooth, like a giant hand rubbing sandpaper across the land. The hills you see today are the parts that were tough enough to survive all of that.

And here is the best bit. People long ago noticed that a big steep rock is a brilliant place to build a castle, because enemies cannot climb up it. So that is exactly what they did. The city grew up around its volcanoes. And today you get to climb one of them yourself. Ready?
