# Your Train Is in a Secret Race
Here's something you probably didn't know about the exact train you're sitting on. It's in a race. Not a race you can see out of the window, but a real one, going on all the time, and your train is one of the racers.

Your train is called a Lumo. And on this same railway line there are other trains, run by a different company, painted a different colour, and the two of them are competing to see who can carry people up to Scotland the best. But here's the really surprising part. The train your family is actually trying to beat isn't the other train at all. It's the aeroplanes. High up in the sky above you, planes are flying people from London to Edinburgh too. And your Lumo train is trying to prove that going by train is better than flying. No airport, no waiting around in long queues taking your shoes off, and you get to sit and watch castles and the sea roll by, instead of just looking at clouds. Plus, if you book your tickets early, the train can be much, much cheaper than a plane. That's the whole clever idea behind Lumo. Be cheap, be comfy, and beat the planes.

Now feel how quiet and smooth your train is. That's a special trick too. This train doesn't burn any fuel at all, not a single drop. It doesn't puff out any smoke like the old steam engines did. Instead, it's completely electric, and it gets all its power from a wire strung up high above the track, the way a toy racing car gets power from the track underneath it. Look up next time you're near a window and you might spot the wires. That's what's pushing you along at about a hundred and twenty-five miles an hour, silently.

And because it doesn't burn any smoky fuel, your train is also much kinder to the planet than flying. When a plane flies to Scotland, it puffs out huge clouds of dirty gas high into the sky. Your electric train doesn't do that at all. So every time a family chooses the train instead of the plane, the air stays a little bit cleaner. You're not just racing the planes, you're beating them at being tidy, too. That's a race worth winning.

And here's a fact to make you smile. This very train was partly built in England, in a big factory up in the north that you'll actually go past later today, but the design came all the way from Japan. So you're riding a super-quiet, super-fast, half-Japanese electric racer, zooming up the same tracks where steam engines raced each other more than a hundred years ago. The race to Scotland has been going on for a very, very long time. And right now, you're winning it.
