# So What Does a King Even Do?

If the king doesn't make the rules, you might be wondering, well, what does he actually do all day? Sit around eating cake off golden plates? Not quite. His job is enormous, and busy, and it is all about being the star of the country rather than the boss of it.

Here is one of his real jobs, and it is a funny one. Every single new law that Parliament makes has to be shown to the king, and he has to say yes to it before it can happen. Sounds powerful, right? But here is the catch. He always says yes. Always. No king has said no for more than three hundred years, because everyone agrees that saying no would break the whole deal. So he does have to sign, and he also has to sign. Both things are true at once. Funny, isn't it?

Once a year he does something amazing. He climbs into a golden coach, pulled by horses, wears a crown so heavy it could give you a headache, and rides to Parliament to read out a big list of all the new laws for the year. But get this. He didn't write a single word of it. The government wrote it, and he just reads it out loud, like a newsreader in a crown.

The rest of the time, he does the things only a king can do. Once a week he has a private chat with the prime minister, where he is allowed to give advice but never allowed to give orders. He hands out shiny medals to brave people and clever people and even famous singers. He welcomes kings and presidents from other countries. He visits hospitals and plants trees and waves, so much waving.

And then there is the pure sparkly showing off, and honestly it is the best part. The soldiers in bright red coats and enormous fuzzy black hats. The Changing of the Guard. The family stepping out onto that balcony while a huge crowd roars below. None of it makes a single rule. But all of it says, loud and proud, this is our country, and it has been here a very, very long time.
