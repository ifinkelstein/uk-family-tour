# The Museum Anyone Can Walk Into
Let me tell you how this whole enormous place began, because it started with one man who simply could not stop collecting things.

His name was Hans Sloane, and he was a doctor who lived a very long time ago, more than three hundred years back. Sloane collected everything. Shells, bones, strange dried plants, coins, books, and weird objects from faraway lands. By the time he was an old man he had gathered more than seventy thousand things. Seventy thousand! Imagine a house so crammed with treasures that you can barely squeeze through the hallway.

Now, most rich collectors back then kept their treasures secret, tucked away in fancy private rooms, and only their posh friends ever got a peek. But when Sloane grew old, he had a different idea. He said the whole gigantic collection should belong to the whole country, and, this is the important bit, that ordinary people should be allowed to come and see it for free.

And that is exactly what happened. In 1759 the doors opened, and the rule was written down in old fashioned words that basically meant this, anyone curious is welcome. Rich or poor. Young or old. From nearby or from the other side of the world. That was a brand new idea back then, and honestly it was a little bit revolutionary.

So when you walk in today, remember this. You are not sneaking in, and you are not just a lucky guest. You have a right to be here. A doctor who lived three hundred years ago wanted a curious kid exactly like you to be able to stand nose to nose with a real Egyptian mummy, without paying a single coin. Pretty amazing gift, right?
