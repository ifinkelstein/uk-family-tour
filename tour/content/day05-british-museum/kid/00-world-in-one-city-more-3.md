# How to Beat a Giant Museum
One more thing before you charge in, and it might be the most useful tip of the whole day. This museum is enormous. It holds around eight million objects. Eight million! If you tried to look at every single one for just a few seconds each, you would be here for months, and your feet would probably fall right off.

So here is the secret that real explorers know. Do not try to see everything. You cannot. Nobody can. Instead, be a treasure hunter. Pick a few brilliant rooms, choose a mission in each one, and leave while you are still having fun. It is far better to truly meet a handful of amazing things than to trudge past a thousand and remember none of them.

If you do just one thing this morning, go and find the Egyptians, the giant stone kings and the mummies wrapped in bandages. Everything else is a bonus. A little later, we will hand you a proper treasure map with the best rooms on it.

And here is the shape of your whole day. In the morning, at this museum, you travel through the ancient world and back in time thousands of years. Then in the afternoon, at the National Gallery, you travel through hundreds of years of paintings, all the way up to swirly, sunny pictures by an artist named Van Gogh. Morning, the world. Afternoon, the pictures of the world.

Last tip. Let yourself be surprised. Keep your eyes peeled for treasures nobody told you to look for. A two headed snake covered in tiny blue stones. A knight so angry that he is biting his own shield. A golden face pulled from a buried ship. The very best moments in a museum are almost always the ones you bump into by accident.
