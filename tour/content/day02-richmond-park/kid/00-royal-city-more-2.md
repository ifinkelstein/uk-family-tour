# The Family That Shaped a Whole Country
Here is something important to know before this trip really begins. For hundreds and hundreds of years, kings and queens did not just wear crowns and wave from balconies. They were the bosses of the entire country. Whatever a king wanted, a king mostly got. And that is exactly why so many of the amazing places you are about to see even exist.

Take this very park. A king named Charles the First wanted somewhere close to London to go hunting, so he simply built a giant wall all the way around this land and said, mine now. The people who lived and farmed here were not pleased about it one bit, but he was the king, so the deer moved in, and the park is still here today. One person's wish, still shaping the ground under your feet almost four hundred years later.

So here is your mission for the whole trip: become a royal detective. Everywhere we go, ask yourself, did a king or a queen make this happen? Very often, the answer is yes. There is a mighty castle in London called the Tower, built by a conqueror to make sure everyone knew who was in charge. There is a spectacular palace where a king named Henry the Eighth grabbed the finest house in the land, just because he liked it. And far away to the north, in Scotland, there is a totally different set of kings and queens with their own dramatic castles perched high on cliffs, in places called Edinburgh and Stirling.

Some of these royals were brave, some were clever, and quite a lot of them were, honestly, pretty terrible to the people around them. But they had the power and the gold to build enormous things, and those enormous things are exactly what we are going to explore. So start counting the kings and queens today, right here. By the end of the trip, you will know this royal family better than you ever expected to.
