# The Vikings Are Coming — And Staying
On the first of November, 866, York's people were celebrating All Saints' Day when a Viking army — the Great Heathen Army, which is what people actually called it — swept into the city and took it. But here's the twist most people don't know: the Vikings didn't just raid and leave. They liked York. They stayed, settled down, and turned it into Jorvik, one of the richest trading cities in Europe. For nearly a century, York was ruled by Viking kings with fantastic names — the last one was Eric Bloodaxe, who sounds terrifying and was. Viking Jorvik was crowded, busy and international: archaeologists have found silk from Central Asia, coins from Samarkand, amber from the Baltic, and a cowrie shell from the Red Sea — all in the mud of York. Down at the JORVIK Centre, you'll ride a slow "time car" through a rebuilt Viking street from exactly one thousand years ago, with houses, workshops, voices speaking Old Norse — and smells. Yes, real, engineered smells: woodsmoke, fish, leather, and the unmistakable whiff of the Viking toilet pit. The faces of some figures were even reconstructed from real Viking skulls found right here. And remember: this isn't a made-up street. It's built exactly where the real one stood, based on what came out of the ground beneath your feet.
