# Your First Morning in London
Good morning, and welcome to London! Today is your first real day here, so stop for a second and look around, because you are standing in a very old, very surprising place. London is about two thousand years old. Two thousand! It was started by the Romans, the ones with the shields and the sandals, long before knights, long before castles. People have lived here without stopping ever since. If every year were a single step, you would have to take two thousand steps to walk all the way back to the very beginning.

Now here is the surprising bit. London is a giant, busy city, one of the biggest in the world, full of red buses and tall towers and millions of people. And yet you are beginning your visit in a wild green park with actual deer wandering around in it. Real ones. That is because London is secretly one of the greenest cities anywhere, sprinkled with enormous parks, and this one, Richmond Park, is the biggest of them all.

Why deer, though? Because hundreds of years ago a king wanted a place to go hunting, so he took all this land for himself. Kings and queens are going to pop up again and again on this trip, in castles and palaces and even on a hill you will climb later today, all the way from London up to a city called Edinburgh and another called York. So keep your eyes open. The story starts right here, under these gigantic old trees, with the deer keeping one careful eye on you.
