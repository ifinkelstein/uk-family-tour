# The Most Famous Poo in the World
You knew this was coming. JORVIK's most celebrated treasure — genuinely, scientifically celebrated — is a fossilized Viking poo. It's called the Lloyds Bank Coprolite, because it was found in 1972 under the site of a Lloyds Bank branch, and it is magnificent: about twenty centimeters long, the largest fossilized human dropping ever found. Scientists have studied it very seriously, and it's a gold mine of information. From it we know its maker ate mostly meat and bread, not many vegetables — and that they were infested with hundreds of parasitic worms, so their tummy ache was probably historic too. One scientist famously described the coprolite as being as precious, in its own way, as the Crown Jewels — a sentence he must regret at parties. In 2003, disaster struck: the coprolite was dropped during a school visit and broke into three pieces. It was carefully glued back together by experts, which is surely one of the strangest jobs in the history of museum conservation. Find it in its display case and pay your respects. Here's the real lesson hiding in the joke: kings write history books about themselves, but ordinary people leave behind rubbish, lost shoes, and yes, poo — and that's how we truly know how people lived. Science means studying everything. Everything.
