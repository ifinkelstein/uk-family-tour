# The Baby Who Was Crowned King
Come inside this old, old church. It is called the Church of the Holy Rude, and it is the oldest building in the whole town except for the castle. Look straight up. See that dark wooden roof? It was built about six hundred years ago, and it's held together with wooden pegs, like the world's biggest, cleverest jigsaw puzzle. No nails at all. And it has never once fallen down.

Now for the big story. Remember how, up in the castle, you heard that Mary, Queen of Scots was crowned when she was just a baby? Well, Mary grew up and became a real queen. But then something sad happened. Her enemies took her crown away and locked her up. And they decided to make somebody else king instead. Guess who? Her own little boy, just over one year old. A baby in nappies.

So right here, in this very church, in the year 1567, they crowned a baby as King of Scotland. His name was James, and one day he would be king of England too. Imagine it. A wriggling one year old, a crown far too big for his head, and a grumpy old preacher called John Knox giving a very long, very serious speech, while the baby probably just wanted his lunch.

So think about this for a second. Mary was crowned as a baby up in the castle. Her son was crowned as a baby down here in the town. Same family, same little hill, and both of them too young even to walk.

When we go back outside, look at the graveyard on the slope, hundreds of tilting old stones, some so old the writing has worn clean away. One more funny fact about the church. For almost three hundred years, a wall ran right down the middle inside, splitting it into two churches, because two groups of people had an argument and didn't want to share. Imagine building a wall down the middle of your house just because you fell out with your sister.
