# The Tiny Creature That Burps
Meet yeast. It is a living thing so small you could fit thousands of them on this full stop, and it has one favourite food in the whole world. Sugar. When yeast finds sugar, it gobbles it up, and then, there is no polite way to say this, it burps. Billions and billions of tiny burps of gas. Every bubble in bread, every bubble in real beer, is a yeast burp. You have been eating yeast burps your whole life, every time you eat a slice of toast. You're welcome.

So how do you make beer? You start with barley, a grain like wheat. The barley gets soaked in water and tricked into starting to sprout, as if spring has come, because sprouting fills the grain with sugar. Then it's dried and toasted. Now it's called malt, and it smells wonderful, like biscuits. The brewer grinds it up and stirs it into a giant pot of hot water, like making the world's biggest cup of barley tea. The sweet liquid that comes out even tastes like the milk left at the bottom of your cereal bowl. Brewers give it a funny old name, the wort. Say it like "wert."

Next the brewer boils the wort and throws in hops. A hop is a flower from a climbing plant, a little green papery cone, like a pinecone made of tissue paper, and it tastes amazingly bitter. That sounds like a mistake, but it's like putting lemon in something sweet. It balances the taste, and it helps stop the beer going bad.

Then comes the yeast's big moment. The brewer pours the cooled sweet liquid into a huge tank and adds the yeast, and the feast begins. For days the whole tank froths and fizzes and heaves like a bubble bath gone wild, while billions of yeast eat the sugar and burp out bubbles. The burping also makes the part that turns it into a grown-up drink, the alcohol. In a bakery, the same creature does the same trick in bread dough, the burps puff the bread up, and the oven's heat cooks everything else away. Same tiny creature. Bread or beer just depends on what you do with its burps.

One more secret, and you might remember it from the whisky story. If you take this bubbly barley liquid before the hops go in, and boil it in one of those giant copper kettles to catch the strong part, you're on the way to making whisky. So beer is really whisky's big sibling. Beer came first, thousands of years first, and whisky is what happens when beer gets boiled down and put to bed in a barrel for years.

So next time you see bread rising, or spot those bubbles in a glass, you'll know the truth. Somewhere in there, a crowd of the tiniest pets in the world just finished their dinner, and they all burped at once.
