# The Real Vikings Were Wilder Than the Cartoons
Today is Viking day, and you are standing on the exact ground where they built their greatest city in England. So let's meet them properly, because the cartoons get almost everything wrong. Here is the first surprise. The Vikings were not one country. They were seafaring people from the cold lands we now call Norway, Sweden and Denmark, and it all kicked off on one terrible morning. On the eighth of June, in the year 793, Viking ships came out of nowhere and attacked a peaceful island monastery just up the coast from here, robbing it and terrifying everyone in it. People were so shocked that the news raced across the whole of Europe. And that was only the beginning. Now here is the second surprise, and it is the big one. The Vikings were not just raiders. The very same people were also brilliant traders, explorers and adventurers. In their long wooden ships they sailed almost unbelievable distances. They crossed a whole ocean to Iceland, then to icy Greenland, and then, a thousand years ago, all the way to North America, five hundred years before a man named Columbus. They rowed rivers deep into Russia. A few even ended up guarding an emperor in a faraway golden city. And right here in York, they did not just attack and leave. They stayed, and built a busy, crowded, smelly, wonderful city called Jorvik. In a moment you will ride straight through it. But first, one fact you can bust today with total confidence. Those horns on the Viking helmet in every cartoon? Completely made up. Not one single real Viking ever wore them. Keep that ready, because the museum is about to prove it.
