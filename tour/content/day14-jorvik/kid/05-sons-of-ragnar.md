# The Sons of Ragnar

You already know that a big Viking army marched in and took York way back in the year eight sixty-six. But who was actually in charge of that army? The old Viking stories give us a wonderful answer, and it comes with one of the best Viking names ever.

The stories say the army was led by a band of brothers, the sons of a famous Viking chief named Ragnar Lothbrok, which means Ragnar Hairy-Breeches, because he supposedly wore funny shaggy trousers into battle. And the most famous brother of all had a name that will give you the shivers. He was called Ivar the Boneless. Nobody today is quite sure why. Maybe he could not walk and was carried into battle on a shield. Maybe he was just amazingly bendy and quick. Either way, Ivar the Boneless and his brothers were said to be the fiercest, cleverest Viking leaders of their whole age, and the stories say they came to England on a mission of revenge, hunting for the king who had killed their dad.

Now, some of that is true history and some of it is a thrilling story the Vikings told each other around the fire hundreds of years later, and it can be tricky to tell which is which. So next let me tell you the legend of what happened to their father Ragnar, and it involves a king, a pit full of snakes, and a very rude last laugh. Ready?
