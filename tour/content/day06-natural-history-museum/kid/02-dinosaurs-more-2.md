# The Dinner Inside a Dinosaur
Here is one of the strangest true stories in the whole history of dinosaurs. On the last night of the year eighteen fifty-three, a group of important gentlemen in top hats sat down to a fancy dinner, with soup and roast and singing. And they ate it sitting inside a dinosaur.

Let me explain. Long before this museum was built, people had only just realised that dinosaurs had ever existed at all. So a sculptor named Benjamin Waterhouse Hawkins was given an amazing job. He was asked to build the very first life-sized dinosaur models the world had ever seen, out of brick, iron, and concrete, for a great glass building in south London called the Crystal Palace. The scientist Richard Owen, the same man who later helped create this museum, told him what the animals should look like.

To make the giant Iguanodon, Hawkins first built a hollow mould, a bit like an enormous empty shell. And before he filled it, he had a wonderful idea. He set a table for about twenty guests inside the mould of the Iguanodon and threw a New Year's Eve party in there. Owen sat at the head of the table, right where the dinosaur's head would be. They ate a feast of many courses and sang songs late into the night, tucked inside a concrete dinosaur.

Now here is the funny part. Owen and Hawkins got a lot of it wrong. Nobody yet understood these animals properly, so they built the Iguanodon as a big lumpy creature on four legs, like a giant lizard, with a pointed spike stuck on its nose like a rhinoceros horn. Years later scientists worked out that the spike was not a nose horn at all. It was a thumb. And Iguanodon did not lump along on all fours the way they thought.

So the Crystal Palace dinosaurs are, in a way, a giant beautiful mistake. But people love them anyway. They are still standing in a park in London today, more than a hundred and seventy years later, so old and so important that they are now specially protected, like a precious building. You can go and visit them, these funny, wrong, wonderful concrete monsters, the great-great-grandparents of every dinosaur model you have ever seen. And you can stand right beside the very Iguanodon that once had a dinner party inside its belly.
