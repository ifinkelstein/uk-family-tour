# An Earthquake, a Giant Sloth, and Stardust
Three more wonders. First, in the volcanoes and earthquakes zone, stand in the earthquake room: a mock-up of a Japanese supermarket that shakes beneath your feet, replaying the real 1995 Kobe earthquake while the shelves rattle around you. Second, hunt for the giant ground sloth skeleton — a sloth the size of an elephant that lived in South America until just a few thousand years ago. Charles Darwin dug up bones of giant sloths on his famous voyage, and puzzling over creatures like these helped lead him to his theory of evolution. The museum owns thousands of specimens Darwin collected himself. Third, in the Minerals gallery and the Vault, find the meteorites — actual rocks from space, older than the Earth itself. Some contain tiny grains of stardust that formed around other stars before our Sun even existed. You are allowed to say "whoa." And a building secret as you walk around: this whole museum is decorated like a stone zoo. The architect covered the walls, inside and out, with carved animals — monkeys climbing the arches, fish, birds, and creatures on every column. Living animals decorate the west side of the building; extinct ones decorate the east. See how many carved creatures you can spot. Nobody has ever counted them all.
