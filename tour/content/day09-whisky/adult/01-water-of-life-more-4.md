# Where to Find a Dram in Edinburgh
So, having heard the whole story, where does a curious grown-up actually go to taste it? Edinburgh is spoiled for choice, and the good news is you need not be an expert. You need only be willing.

The classic is probably the Bow Bar, on steep and colourful Victoria Street just below the castle. It looks as though it has stood there forever, a snug traditional room with no music and no fuss, and behind the bar rises a magnificent tall wooden gantry, said to have been rescued from an old church, lined with more than three hundred whiskies. They do proper Scotch pies too, so you can eat while you dither. If you fancy something a little more polished, walk up to the Whiski Rooms near the top of the Mound, almost in the shadow of the castle, a smart bar and restaurant with a shop attached, where they run friendly tutored tastings and will happily marry a dram to food, or to cheese, or to chocolate.

For the true enthusiast there is the Tipsy Midgie, a tiny, cosy den that was named Scotland's best whisky bar two years running, in twenty twenty-three and again in twenty twenty-four, with getting on for a thousand bottles crammed behind its little bar and a team who love nothing better than steering you toward something new. And for a bit of theatre, hunt down Usquabae, whose name is simply the old Gaelic for water of life, a dark and handsome cellar bar down in the vaults with four hundred whiskies and snug private booths where you can settle in for the evening.

One important word for anyone new to this. Do not start with the smoky monsters of Islay, or you may never come back. Begin with something gentle and fruity, a Speyside perhaps, and ask the bartender, who will be glad you did. Take it slowly. Add a drop of water. Smell before you sip. A dram is not a thing to knock back but a thing to sit with.

And if the whole family is together, one place welcomes everyone, up on Castlehill by the castle. The Scotch Whisky Experience. It is built for curious visitors of every age, with a gentle barrel ride through the making of whisky and the astonishing Diageo Claive Vidiz collection, the largest gathering of Scotch whisky in the world, well over three thousand bottles glowing on the shelves like jewels. The children get the ride and the smells and the wide-eyed wonder. Afterwards, the grown-ups can slip off to one of those bars and raise a well-earned glass. Slàinte mhath. Good health.
