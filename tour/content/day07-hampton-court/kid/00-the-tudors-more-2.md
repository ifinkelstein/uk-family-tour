# The King Who Started His Own Church
Now here is the biggest thing Henry the Eighth ever did, and it all started because of an argument about who he was married to. When Henry was young, he married a Spanish princess named Catherine. But after many years, they had a daughter and no son, and Henry desperately wanted a son to be king after him. He also wanted to marry someone new. So he asked for special permission to end his first marriage.

Back then, one single person was in charge of the whole church across most of Europe. He lived far away in Rome, in Italy, and he was called the Pope. If you wanted to end a marriage, you had to ask him first. So Henry asked. And the Pope said no.

Now, most people would just accept that. Not Henry. Henry got so angry that he made an incredible decision. He said, fine, then I will not listen to the Pope at all, ever again. In a law passed in fifteen thirty-four, he announced that he, the King of England, was now the head of his very own separate church, called the Church of England. No Pope allowed.

Think about how bold that is. Imagine being told no, and instead of giving up, you go off and start your own everything. That is basically what Henry did with an entire country's religion. This whole enormous change was part of something grown-ups call the Reformation, a time when lots of people all across Europe were breaking away from the old church and starting new ones.

And here is the wildest part. It stuck. To this very day, the King or Queen of England is still the head of the Church of England, all because Henry the Eighth wanted a different wife. Whether you think that was a good reason or a silly one, you have to admit, it is one of the most surprising ways a country ever changed. One man's family argument, and a whole nation's religion turned upside down.
