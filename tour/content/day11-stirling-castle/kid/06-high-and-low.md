# Who Really Lived Here?
You have heard about kings and queens, a baby being crowned, a man who tried to fly, and a castle full of cannons. Now let me tell you a different kind of story. Let me tell you about all the other people who lived and worked on this rock, from the fanciest person right down to the poorest, and what an ordinary day was really like for each of them.

Think of the people of long-ago Scotland as a giant ladder. Right at the very top, on the highest rung, stood one single person, the king or the queen. Just below them stood the powerful lords and their clever advisers, the rich and important families who helped run the country. Below them came the skilled workers, the builders and carvers and painters, the people who actually made this castle beautiful with their own hands. And down at the bottom, on the widest rung of all, stood the farmers, ordinary families who grew the food. There were far, far more of them than anyone else.

Here is the interesting part. Every single one of these people was connected to this one castle. The farmer grew the grain, and the grain became the king's bread. The builder carved the stone, and the stone became the king's window. Nobody could manage without the others, not even the king.

So over the next few little stories, we are going to climb up and down that ladder together. We will spend a day as a king, meet a mighty lord, watch the builders sweat, sit down to a poor family's dinner, and finally meet one very interesting person you have not heard about yet. Ready? Let's start climbing.
