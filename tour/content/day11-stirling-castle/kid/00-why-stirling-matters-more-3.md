# One Family and a Whole Lot of Castles
Here is a fun thing to notice on our trip. The same royal family keeps popping up, again and again, in different castles and cities. And a lot of them are connected right here at Stirling.

The family were called the Stewarts. They were the kings and queens of Scotland, and this castle was their favourite home. They grew up here, got married here, and raised their own children here, safe behind the strong walls.

The most famous was Mary, Queen of Scots. She became queen when she was just a tiny baby, only a few days old. She was crowned in the chapel here when she was nine months old. Imagine a baby being made queen of a whole country.

Now for the amazing part. Mary had a son named James. Far away in England, there was a queen called Elizabeth, but she never had any children. So when Elizabeth died, guess who got to be the next king of England? Mary's son, James. He was already King of Scotland, and now he became King of England too. One person, wearing two crowns, joining the two countries together for the first time.

That is why this family shows up everywhere we go, in London, in Edinburgh, and here. If you look at the Royal Family Tree in this app, this is the family it is all about. One big, dramatic, castle hopping family.
