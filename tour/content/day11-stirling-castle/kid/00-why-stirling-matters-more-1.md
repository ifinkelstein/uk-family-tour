# Two Countries Sharing One Island
Here is something that surprises a lot of visitors. Today, England and Scotland are part of one country called the United Kingdom. But for a very, very long time, they were two completely separate countries, sharing one island.

Think of it like this. Imagine one big island with a line drawn across the middle. On the bottom half lived the English, with their own king. On the top half lived the Scots, with their own king and their own crown. England was bigger, and had more people and more money. Scotland was smaller, with lots of mountains, which actually made it harder for anyone to conquer.

Sometimes the two sides got along, and their royal families even married each other. But a lot of the time, they fought. Again and again, powerful English kings tried to take over Scotland and rule it themselves. One English king, Edward the First, was so tough on the Scots that they nicknamed him the Hammer of the Scots. He even stole the special stone that Scottish kings sat on to be crowned.

That made the Scots furious, and they fought back. Two brave leaders became heroes for standing up to England. One was William Wallace. The other was Robert the Bruce. You will meet both of them here at Stirling, because this castle was right in the middle of all the fighting.

So whenever you hear about battles and castles today, remember, it is really a story about two countries, sharing one island, who could not stop arguing over who was in charge.
