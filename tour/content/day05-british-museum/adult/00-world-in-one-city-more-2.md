# How the World Ended Up in London
Here is the honest part of the story, and it is worth having straight before you fall in love with the objects. How did the treasures of so many other places come to sit in a museum in England?

The answer is a mix, and the mix matters. Some things were bought, openly and legally, from collectors and dealers. Some were gifts, or the fruit of scientific expeditions and patient archaeology. And a great deal of it arrived on the back of British power in the age of empire, when the map was colored with a good deal of pink and the Royal Navy ruled the sea lanes. When your country runs a quarter of the globe, the world's objects have a way of flowing toward your capital. Diplomats, soldiers, and administrators shipped home statues, manuscripts, and whole temple facades, sometimes with local permission, sometimes with permission that is fiercely disputed, and sometimes as the plain spoils of war.

You will meet this tension directly today. The Rosetta Stone, which you will reach upstairs shortly, was found by French soldiers in Egypt and then handed to the British as a prize of war, and both France and Egypt have feelings about that. The Parthenon sculptures, the great marbles from the temple in Athens, were removed by a British lord in the early eighteen hundreds under a permission whose exact wording no longer survives, and Greece has wanted them back for two centuries. These are not settled questions. Museums across Europe are returning objects, courts and parliaments are arguing, and the old defense, that a single universal museum lets you see all of human culture side by side, is being tested and renegotiated in real time.

You do not have to resolve any of it today. But it makes the day richer, not poorer, to hold two things at once, that these objects are breathtaking, and that many of them carry a difficult history of how they got here. We will point to the sharpest cases as we reach them. For now, just keep the question in your pocket, who made this, who took it, and who should keep it.
