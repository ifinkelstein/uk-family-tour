# The Oldest Game of Skittles
Inside that ancient sheep's head inn there is something wonderful, and it's a game. It's called skittles, and it might just be the oldest one of its kind still being played anywhere in Scotland. If you've ever been ten-pin bowling, you already know how it works, because skittles is bowling's great-great-great-grandparent.

Here's the game. At the far end of a long narrow lane, someone stands up nine wooden pins, called skittles, in a neat little pattern. Then, from the other end, you roll a heavy ball, or sometimes throw a wooden disc, straight down the lane and try to knock over as many pins as you can. Knock them all down and everyone cheers. Miss, and the ball rolls sadly off to the side while everyone groans. It's simple, it's noisy, and people have been playing it for a very, very long time.

Now here's the amazing part. Kings and queens didn't just visit this inn to eat. They played this exact game in the yard behind it, hundreds of years ago. There's a story that King James, the same one who gave the sheep's head snuff box, rolled a few balls down the lane himself and had such fun that he handed over that present. So when children play skittles there today, they're playing the same game a king once played, on the same old spot, all those centuries ago.

The skittle lane they use now is well over a hundred years old, and the wooden pins they knock down are antiques, older than your great-grandparents. Imagine that. In most games you'd be careful with something that old and put it behind glass in a museum. Here, they still bowl balls straight at it for fun, the way people always have.

Think about how many hands have rolled a ball down that lane. Farmers, sailors, grannies, soldiers, kings, and children just like you, all the way back through the years, all trying to knock down the same kind of little wooden pins and all doing a happy little dance when they managed it. Some games come and go, but this one has stuck around for hundreds of years, because knocking things down with a ball is just brilliant, and it always will be.

So next time you go bowling, do a tiny bit of showing off and tell everyone you know where the game came from. An ancient inn in Scotland, at the foot of a volcano, named after a sheep.
