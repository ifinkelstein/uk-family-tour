# The Man Who Helped the King on the Toilet
Ready for the strangest job in the whole palace? Henry the Eighth did not use the big shared toilet, obviously. A king had his own private one, a special padded seat called a close stool. It was a wooden box with a hole in the top and a pot underneath, and here is the surprise, it was covered in soft velvet, studded with gold nails, and fitted with the finest cushions, because even going to the toilet, a king had to do it in luxury.

But somebody had to look after all this. Somebody had to carry the close stool from room to room, keep it clean, bring the water and the cloths, and, yes, help the king himself, and then take everything away and empty it. That person had a job title, and I am not making this up. He was called the Groom of the Stool. Stool was an old word for toilet.

Now, you would think that would be the worst, most embarrassing job at court, the one nobody wanted. Wrong. It was one of the most powerful jobs in the entire kingdom. Think about why. The Groom of the Stool was with the king at his most private moments, every single day. Nobody else got that much time alone with him. So the king talked to him, trusted him, told him secrets, and asked his advice. If you wanted the king to hear about something, you might quietly have a word with the Groom of the Stool. He even ended up looking after huge piles of the king's money. Grown men, rich and important lords, were jealous of the fellow who emptied the royal potty.

One of Henry's Grooms of the Stool was a man named Henry Norris, and his story shows just how close, and how dangerous, this job really was. Norris was one of Henry's absolute favourites, trusted with everything. But when the king turned against his second wife, Anne Boleyn, Norris was accused of being far too friendly with her, swept up in the plot, and executed alongside her in the year fifteen thirty-six. So the man who was closest of all to the king, closer even than most of the royal family, lost his head. At Henry's court, being near the king could make you powerful, and being near the king could get you killed. Sometimes, it was both at once.
