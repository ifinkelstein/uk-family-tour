# Things to Spot Before We Start Walking
Before we start walking, here is your spotting list, because a park is way more exciting when you know exactly what to look for. Ready?

First, look how wild it is out here. You are only about eight miles from the middle of a gigantic city, but it feels like proper countryside, with long grass, prickly bracken, and enormous old trees. See those big, twisty oak trees? Some of them are hundreds and hundreds of years old, older than the park, even older than the whole country of America. Put your hand on one if you can reach it, and think about everything it has quietly stood through while you touch its bark.

Second, here is a secret about London. It is one of the greenest big cities in the entire world, stuffed full of parks. And do you know why? Kings and queens. Long ago they grabbed loads of land just for themselves, to hunt and play in, and much, much later those royal grounds became parks that everyone is allowed to enjoy. So this giant green space was once a king's private playground, and now it belongs to you for the whole morning.

Third, use your ears and eyes like a real explorer. Listen high up for a skylark, a tiny bird that sings and sings while it hovers in the sky like it is showing off just for you. Feel the wind pushing through the bracken. And sweep your eyes across the grass for shapes that might just turn out to be deer, always spotted from far, far away, calm and quiet.

And here is one thing to look forward to. A little later we will climb a hill with an amazing secret hidden inside a hedge, where you can peep all the way across London to a giant cathedral, ten whole miles away. But that is for later. Right now, take a big breath of this green, wild air. This is the start of your adventure. Let us go!
