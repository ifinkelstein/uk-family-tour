# The Hotel Room Where the Story Ended

Every story has to end somewhere. Harry Potter's ending has an exact address, and you can walk right past it. Find the grand hotel with the huge clock tower at the east end of Princes Street, next to the train station. That's the Balmoral. Count up to the fifth floor. Behind one of those windows, the last Harry Potter book was finished.

Here's how it happened. By two thousand and seven, Rowling wasn't a broke young mum in a cafe anymore. She was one of the most famous writers on Earth, and she was fighting to finish the seventh and final book, Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows. Millions of readers were waiting. And at home, there was always noise, always something. So she packed up her work and checked into room five five two at the Balmoral, just to get some peace and finish the thing.

The room won. On the eleventh of January, two thousand and seven, in that room, she wrote the final words of the final chapter of the final book. Seventeen years after Harry first appeared in her head on a delayed train, the story was done.

And then she did something you are absolutely not supposed to do in a hotel. In the room stood a marble bust, a statue of the Greek god Hermes. Rowling picked up a pen and wrote right on it. She wrote that J K Rowling finished writing Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows in this room, five five two, on the eleventh of January, two thousand and seven. She signed the statue like it was a giant autograph book. If you drew on the furniture in a fancy hotel, you'd be in enormous trouble. When the most famous author in the world does it, the hotel throws a party.

And the Balmoral did something wonderful next. Instead of scrubbing the statue clean, they put it under a glass dome to protect it, and turned the whole room into the J K Rowling Suite. The door was painted purple, and it got a brass door knocker shaped like an owl, like a certain snowy owl you might know. Best of all, the number on the door isn't five five two anymore. It's five five two and three quarters. If you know how to get to Platform Nine and Three Quarters, you know exactly why.

People now pay well over a thousand pounds a night to sleep in the room where Harry Potter ended. Or you can do what we're doing, stand outside for free, look up at the fifth floor, and imagine the exact moment a pen stopped moving and the biggest story of our time was finally complete.
