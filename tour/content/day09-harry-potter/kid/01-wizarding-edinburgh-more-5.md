# Hogwarts Is in Scotland

Where is Hogwarts, really? Not the film set, not a theme park. In the books, where is the castle supposed to be? The answer is all around you. Hogwarts is in Scotland.

Think about how students get there. They board the Hogwarts Express at King's Cross station in London, you may have stood in that exact station, and the train chugs north all day, arriving after dark. Rowling explained it once in an interview. Travel a whole day north from King's Cross and where do you end up? Scotland. She said that even before she lived here, she always imagined Hogwarts in Scotland, a huge castle by a lake, tucked in the Highlands where the maps run out of towns and fill up with mountains.

The filmmakers agreed. When they needed the Hogwarts Express for the movies, they used a real steam train called the Jacobite, which still runs today across the west of Scotland, on one of the most beautiful railway lines in the world. In the films, you see it thunder across a giant curved bridge with twenty-one stone arches, high above a green valley. That bridge is real. It's the Glenfinnan Viaduct, and the flying car scene in Chamber of Secrets happens right above it. Every day in summer, crowds hike up the hillside just to watch the steam train curl across those arches, whistle echoing, exactly like the start of a new school year. The lakes in the films are real too. Deep, dark Highland lochs played the Great Lake below Hogwarts, and a tiny island on one of them became Dumbledore's white tomb.

Now here's where you come in. You're spending days in Scotland on this trip, and that means you can hunt for your own Hogwarts. Tomorrow-ish you'll ride a boat to Inchcolm, a little island with a ruined abbey sitting alone in the sea. Grey stone towers, screaming seabirds, water on every side. Doesn't that sound like something out of the wizarding world? At Stirling you'll walk through a real castle where real kings and queens lived, with great halls big enough for a Yule Ball. And when you climb Arthur's Seat, the old volcano above Edinburgh, look north across the water toward the mountains. Somewhere out there, says the story, hidden from Muggle eyes, is a castle no map will ever show you.

Remember, Muggles can't see Hogwarts. To them it looks like a mouldering old ruin with a sign saying keep out, danger. So every ruined castle you pass in Scotland, and you'll pass plenty, might secretly be it. Keep score out the window. That's not a broken tower, that's just what they want you to see. By the end of this trip you'll have spotted a dozen possible Hogwartses. One of them, who knows, might be the real thing.
