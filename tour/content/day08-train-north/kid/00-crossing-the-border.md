# The Invisible Line You'll Cross Today
Here is something strange that is going to happen to you today, and you will not feel a thing. Sometime this afternoon, your train is going to leave one country and roll straight into another one. No boat. No bridge over the sea. No airport, no passport, nobody checking anything at all. One minute you will be in England, and a moment later you will be in Scotland, and the only way you would ever know is if somebody told you.

So how does that work? Look at it this way. You are riding up one big island. It has a name, Great Britain, and the funny thing about this island is that it holds more than one country. Down at the bottom and in the middle is England, where you started this morning. Way up at the top is Scotland, where you are heading. And off to the west, where you cannot see it from the train, there is a third one called Wales. Three countries, all sharing one island, like three families living in one very long house.

And they really are different countries, not just different parts of the same place. Long, long ago, England and Scotland each had their own king and their own army, and they fought each other for hundreds of years. Today they share a king and they are the best of friends, but Scotland still does loads of things its own way. Different money. Different laws. Different schools. It even has a different pretend animal as its national symbol, a unicorn, of all things. So keep your eyes on the window. Somewhere out there is an invisible line, and the moment your train crosses it, everything on the other side is Scotland. Your whole week is about to happen up there.
