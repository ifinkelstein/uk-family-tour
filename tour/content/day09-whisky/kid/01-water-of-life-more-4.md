# A Whisky Adventure for the Whole Family
You might be thinking: whisky is for grown-ups, so is there anything fun here for ME? Oh yes. There is a brilliant place right next to Edinburgh Castle, at the very top of the Royal Mile, called the Scotch Whisky Experience, and it is made for curious kids and grown-ups together.

The best bit? You get to ride in a barrel. Really! You climb into a little car shaped like a whisky barrel, and it carries you gently through the whole story of how whisky is made, past the golden barley, the giant copper kettles, the sleeping casks, all the things you have just learned about, right before your eyes. It is a bit like a ride at a theme park, except the theme is the water of life.

Then comes the part I promised you. The smelling. There is a station where you get to sniff different whisky smells and try to guess them. Is that one apples? Is that one smoke? Is that one toffee, or grass, or the sea? Remember, your nose is the real flavour detective, so you can play this game just as well as any grown-up. Maybe better, because kids often have super-powered noses.

And then, wait until you see this. They keep the biggest whisky collection in the entire world, more than three thousand bottles, all lined up and glowing golden on shelves that climb up and up and up like a wall of treasure. Nobody drinks them. They just sit there sparkling, more bottles than you could count in a whole day.

You will not taste any whisky, of course, because that is only for grown-ups, and honestly you would probably say yuck. But while you have been riding barrels and sniffing smells and gasping at the wall of bottles, the grown-ups get to have a tiny taste of a strong golden drink that people have been making in Scotland for more than five hundred years. Later they might slip away to a cosy little bar with a funny name to try another one. You can tell them which smells you found. You are the expert now.

So next time someone says whisky is boring grown-up stuff, tell them the truth. It is barrels and bonfires and angels and smugglers and giant shiny kettles and a wall of three thousand glowing bottles. And that is an adventure for anyone.
