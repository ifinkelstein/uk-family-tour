# Castles by the Sea
This stretch of coast, in a part of England called Northumberland, has more castles than almost anywhere else in the whole country, and from the train you have a real chance of spotting them. So this chapter is a challenge. Become a castle-hunter.

The biggest one to watch for sits on a giant rock right above the beach, above a huge stretch of pale golden sand. It's called Bamburgh Castle, and it is exactly what a castle is supposed to look like, long walls and tall towers standing guard over the sea. Real kings ruled the whole north of England from that rock more than a thousand years ago. Waves crash on the shore below it, and on a windy day the sea spray flies right up the cliff. And just out to sea from here, on a wild stormy night long ago, a brave young woman named Grace Darling rowed a tiny rowing boat out through the crashing waves to rescue drowning sailors from a wrecked ship. She became the most famous girl in the whole country, and people still tell her story nearly two hundred years later.

A little further along, tucked inland, is another famous one you might not see from the train but really should know about. Alnwick Castle. If it looks familiar in photos, that's because it played the part of Hogwarts School in the very first Harry Potter films. The young wizards learned to fly their broomsticks in its courtyard. And here's the best bit. Real children can take actual broomstick-flying lessons there today, which sounds like the greatest school trip ever invented.

And there's a spooky one too, called Dunstanburgh, except this one is a ruin. Just jagged, broken towers standing all alone on an empty headland, with no road to reach them. You have to walk a long way across the fields to get there, past the sheep, and when you finally see it looming up, grey and crumbling against the sky, it looks like exactly the sort of place where a dragon might live.

So here's your mission for the next half hour. Press your nose to the glass and look for anything with stone walls and towers, up on the hills or down by the shore. Every castle counts. Shout it out the moment you spot one. Whoever has counted the most castles by the time you cross the border into Scotland is the official Castle Champion of the entire family. Ready? Eyes on the window. Go.
