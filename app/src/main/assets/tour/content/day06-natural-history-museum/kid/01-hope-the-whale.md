# Hope: The Biggest Animal That Ever Lived
Look up. The skeleton diving toward you through Hintze Hall is Hope, a blue whale — twenty-five meters long, four and a half tonnes of bone. Blue whales are the largest animals that have EVER existed. Bigger than any dinosaur. A blue whale's heart is the size of a small car, and a child could crawl through its biggest blood vessels. Its tongue alone weighs as much as an elephant. And yet it eats some of the smallest creatures in the ocean — krill, little shrimp — about four tonnes of them a day, filtered through bristly plates in its mouth. Hope is a real whale that stranded in Ireland in 1891. She was in the museum's storage and side galleries for over a century before taking the star spot in 2017, replacing Dippy the Diplodocus — which caused a national argument, because Britain loved that dinosaur. But there's a reason she's called Hope: humans hunted blue whales almost to extinction — down to a few thousand left — and then the world agreed to stop, and the whales are slowly coming back. She hangs in a diving position, like she's lunging for krill, as a reminder that we nearly lost the biggest animal in history, and chose not to.
