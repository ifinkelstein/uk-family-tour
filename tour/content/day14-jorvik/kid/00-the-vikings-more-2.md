# You Speak Viking Every Day
Ready for something spooky? You have been speaking Viking words your whole life, and nobody ever told you. Here is how it happened. When the Viking army beat the local kings, the Vikings did not just grab the treasure and sail away. They stayed for good. They shared out the land, married local people, had children, and farmed the fields. So much of England ended up under Viking rule that it got its own special name, the Danelaw, which just means the part of the country where Viking law was in charge instead of English law. And guess which city was the capital of it all. This one. Jorvik, right under your feet. Now for the spooky part. When two groups of people live all mixed up together like that, sharing streets and markets and families, their languages get mixed up too. So little bits of Viking got stuck inside English, and they never came out again. When you say the word sky, that is a Viking word. Egg? Viking. Knife, skin, leg, all Viking. Even the word window is Viking, and it is a good one, because it comes from two Norse words that together mean wind-eye. A window really was an eye for the wind to peek through, back before there was glass. And here is the wildest one of all. The tiny everyday words they, them and their came from the Vikings too. Those are words you use in almost every single sentence you ever say. There is a game you can play from the train window as well. Any town whose name ends in the sound -by, like Whitby or Grimsby, was named by Vikings, because -by was their word for a village. So keep your ears and your eyes wide open today. The Vikings left this place more than a thousand years ago. But little pieces of them are still hiding inside your own mouth, every single time you talk.
