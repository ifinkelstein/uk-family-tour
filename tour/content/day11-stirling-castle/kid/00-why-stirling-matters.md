# Why This Castle Is So Special
Before we go in, let me tell you what makes this place so special, because Stirling might be the most important castle in all of Scotland.

The big secret is this. A long time ago, and for hundreds of years, the island we are standing on was not one country. It was two. Down in the south was England, bigger and richer. Up here in the north was Scotland, with its own kings and its own crown. The two countries argued and fought a lot, a bit like two families sharing one island who could not agree where the fence should go.

Now look down from the castle at the flat land below. Long ago that was squishy, muddy swamp, and Stirling guarded the only safe bridge across the river. That meant any army marching through Scotland had to pass right here, underneath these walls. So people said, whoever holds Stirling holds Scotland. If you controlled this rock, you controlled the whole country.

But Stirling is more than a fort with cannons. It was also a royal home, a fancy painted palace where kings and queens lived. A baby queen was even crowned right here. You will hear all about her later. Her name was Mary, Queen of Scots.

In the next few short chapters, I will help you understand who was fighting who, when all this happened, and how one family connects this castle to almost everywhere else on our trip. Then we will go exploring.
