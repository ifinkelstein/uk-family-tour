# A Feather, the Moon, and a Rock From the Sky
There is a room in this museum full of the rarest treasures of all, and three of them are so amazing they are worth hunting for. The first looks small and grey and easy to walk past, but it may be the most precious fossil in the whole building. It is called Archaeopteryx, and it is the fossil of a creature that is half dinosaur and half bird.

Look closely and you can see feathers pressed into the stone, actual feathers, from an animal that lived about a hundred and fifty million years ago. But this was no ordinary bird. It had sharp teeth in its mouth, claws on its wings, and a long bony tail like a lizard. It is one of the best clues we have ever found that birds are the living cousins of the dinosaurs, that the pigeon on the pavement outside is a tiny, feathery, far-off relative of the dinosaurs in the gallery. This particular fossil was found in Germany in eighteen sixty-one, and it is so famous that scientists simply call it the London specimen, because this is where it lives.

The second treasure is even further from home, because it is not from Earth at all. It is a piece of the Moon. When American astronauts flew to the Moon on the very last Apollo mission, they brought back rocks, and a tiny fragment was given to the United Kingdom as a gift. It is only about the size of your thumbnail, mounted on a little plaque beside a small British flag that actually flew all the way to the Moon and back. So you are looking at something that a human being picked up off the surface of another world.

And the third treasure fell out of the sky all by itself. In seventeen ninety-five, in a field in Yorkshire, a farmworker heard a whistling roar and saw a stone come crashing down out of the clouds, so close that it flung soil into the air. That stone is here. Back then, most people flatly refused to believe that rocks could fall from space at all. This one, called the Wold Cottage meteorite, helped scientists prove that they can, that lumps of rock really do drift through space and sometimes land on Earth.

So in a single room you can meet a feathered dinosaur, rest your eyes on a piece of the Moon, and stand before a rock that fell flaming from the sky. Three treasures, three impossible journeys, all of them ending right here.
