# The Family at the Center of the Story
Before you walk into Hampton Court, it helps to know whose house this really is. For a little over a century, from fourteen eighty-five to sixteen oh three, England was ruled by a single family called the Tudors, and no dynasty in English history left a deeper mark. There were only five of them on the throne, but two loom over everything, and you will meet both of them here today. The first is Henry the Eighth, the enormous, magnetic, dangerous king who took this palace, married six times, and broke England away from the Pope in Rome to get his way. The second is his daughter, Elizabeth the First, who never married at all, ruled for forty-five years, and presided over a burst of confidence, exploration and poetry that people have called a golden age ever since. Shakespeare wrote for her London.

What makes the Tudors matter beyond England is timing. They ruled at the exact moment the medieval world was turning into something recognizably modern. Printed books were spreading, ships were reaching the Americas, and the old unity of Western Christianity was cracking apart into rival churches. The Tudors did not merely live through that upheaval. Henry seized hold of it, and in doing so he made the English monarch the head of a brand new national church, a decision that still shapes British life, law and identity today.

Hampton Court is where you can stand inside their world. It is the greatest surviving Tudor palace anywhere, the place where these people ate, prayed, plotted and slept. Keep them in mind as you explore. Almost everything you will see today was built to impress them, to intimidate them, or to outlast them.
