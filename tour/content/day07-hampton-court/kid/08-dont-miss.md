# The Treasure Hunt List: Don't Miss These
This palace is huge, and nobody sees all of it, so here is your treasure hunt list, the things worth hunting down before your legs give up. Ready? Inside first. The Great Hall, the biggest room in the palace, where you stand under a wooden roof like an upside-down ship and walls hung with tapestries woven with real gold. The Chapel Royal, one glance up at that blue and gold ceiling is enough, it is the best look-up in England. The Haunted Gallery, walk the whole corridor slowly and see if you feel the famous chill. The Tudor kitchens, follow the smell of woodsmoke, and if the fire is lit with real cooks working, drop everything and watch. Now three rooms most visitors march straight past. The Wolsey Closet, a tiny room that glows like the inside of a golden jewellery box, the last little piece of the cardinal's lost palace. The Council Chamber, an ordinary-looking room where a few men around a table made decisions that changed England forever. And in the chapel's royal balcony, on most days from Wednesday to Saturday, a full-size sparkling copy of Henry the Eighth's actual crown, close enough to count the pearls. Then head for the King's Staircase, where the painted walls and ceiling swirl with gods and heroes, and the King's Guard Chamber just beyond it, where close to three thousand real swords, pistols and muskets are arranged on the walls in giant fans and swirls, weapons turned into wallpaper. If you have any museum energy left, peek at the nine enormous paintings of a Roman victory parade, elephants and trumpets included, painted over five hundred years ago by an artist called Mantegna, and the Cumberland Art Gallery next door. Now outside, where the running is allowed. The Maze, obviously. The Magic Garden, obviously. Fountain Court, a perfect circle of grass and water hidden inside the palace like a secret courtyard, do one full lap around it. The Privy Garden, the king's own private garden, so neat it looks ironed. The little Pond Gardens beside it, which look sweet and flowery today but started life as fish tanks, where fresh fish swam around waiting to become Henry's dinner. The Great Vine in its greenhouse, the biggest grapevine on the planet. And the royal tennis court, where, if you are lucky, people in white will be playing the strangest tennis you have ever seen. That is the list. You will not get every single one, nobody does. But every one you find is a point, so keep score, and argue about the winner on the train home.
