# A Volcano and a Station in a Vanished Lake
The last part of your journey is the prettiest of all. After you cross into Scotland, the train hugs the coast again and runs past a little seaside town called Dunbar. Here's a story an American should love. A boy was born in that town, long ago, named John Muir. He grew up climbing the cliffs and poking about in the rock-pools right there. Then he sailed away to America, fell head over heels in love with its wild mountains, and spent the rest of his life persuading people to protect them. Because of John Muir, from tiny Dunbar, America created some of its greatest national parks, including Yosemite, with its towering cliffs and thundering waterfalls. A Scottish boy helped save the wild places of America.

Now watch ahead, because Edinburgh is about to appear, and it appears in the most dramatic way possible. Rising up out of the city is a great green hill with a rocky top. That hill is called Arthur's Seat, and it is an extinct volcano. Don't worry, it hasn't erupted in more than three hundred million years, so you're perfectly safe, but the whole city was built around the bones of an ancient volcano. And on another great chunk of old volcanic rock, a steep black cliff, stands Edinburgh Castle, keeping watch over everything. See if you can spot it up there. You'll get to climb right up to it tomorrow. And here's a lovely bit of Edinburgh weirdness to listen out for: nearly every day, at exactly one o'clock, the castle fires a real cannon, with a great booming bang that rolls out across the whole city, so that everybody can check their watches by it.

Then your train slides into the station, which is called Waverley, and here comes the last surprise. The station sits down in a deep valley in the very middle of the city. But hundreds of years ago, that valley wasn't a valley at all, it was a lake. A smelly, mucky, murky lake called the Nor Loch. The townspeople drained all the water away, and where the lake used to be, they built the railway station and some gardens. So when you step off the train, remember this. You're standing at the bottom of a lake that isn't there anymore, underneath a castle that sits on a volcano. Welcome to Edinburgh. It's going to be brilliant.
