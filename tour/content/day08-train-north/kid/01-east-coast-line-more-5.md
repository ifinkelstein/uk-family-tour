# A Peek at York, Where You'll Be Soon
In the middle of your journey, the train slows down and rolls through a city called York, and I want you to look out for it, because here's the exciting part. In just a few days, you're coming back to spend a whole day here. So think of this as a sneak peek, like watching the trailer before the film.

As the train goes through, press your face to the window and hunt for a truly gigantic church. It's called York Minster, and it is enormous, one of the biggest old churches in the whole of Northern Europe. Look for its tall pale towers poking up above all the rooftops. It took people about two hundred and fifty years to finish building it, stone by stone by stone. That's not two hundred and fifty years ago. That's two hundred and fifty years of actual building, so long that the workers who started it were great-great-great-grandparents by the time it was done, and none of the people who began it ever saw it finished.

York is one of the oldest cities in Britain, and it has had the most amazing lives. The Romans built it as their great fortress city in the north, and a Roman emperor actually died right here. Then, hundreds of years later, the Vikings sailed up the river and captured the whole city and made it their own, and they called it Jorvik. Remember those Viking raiders who attacked Holy Island? These were the same fearsome people, and when you come back to York you'll get to explore a whole underground Viking street, with the sights and even the smells of how they really lived. Yes, the smells. You'll find out.

But that's for later. For now, the train only gives you a quick taste of York before it speeds up again and carries on north. So take your peek, wave hello to that giant church, and remember, York is saving all its best secrets for the day you come back.
