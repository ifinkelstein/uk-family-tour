# The Wizard City

Here is a thought to carry around Edinburgh today. When you rode the train into this city, you did almost exactly what a woman named Joanne Rowling did in December of nineteen ninety-three. She stepped off a train with a baby daughter, very little money, and a suitcase holding something secret. Inside were three chapters about a boy wizard called Harry Potter.

Nobody knew her name back then. She came here because her sister lived in Edinburgh, and she needed somewhere to start over. She pushed her daughter's pram through these streets, and when the baby fell asleep, she ducked into cafes, ordered one coffee, and wrote as fast as she could before the baby woke up. Page by page, in this city, the story you know grew into a book. And fourteen years later, in a fancy hotel room about ten minutes' walk from the castle, she wrote the very last sentence of the very last book. Harry Potter begins and ends in Edinburgh.

And honestly, doesn't this city look the part? A castle sitting on top of a black volcanic rock. Crooked alleyways that dive between buildings like secret passages. A spooky old graveyard where, if you hunt carefully, you can find the name of Lord Voldemort himself carved into a real gravestone.

So here's your mission today. In the stories coming up, you'll visit the cafes where the books got written, and find out which one is fibbing about it. You'll go grave-hunting for Voldemort. You'll walk a curvy rainbow-colored street that people swear is the real Diagon Alley, and hear what the author says about that. You'll peek at the hotel room where the story ended. And you'll find out why Hogwarts itself, according to the books, is hiding somewhere in Scotland right now. Maybe even somewhere you're going this week. Wands ready? Off we go.
