# How Did All This Stuff Get Here?
Here is a puzzle to carry around with you all day. If this is a museum in England, in the city of London, then why is it packed with treasures from Egypt, and Greece, and Iraq, and Africa, and just about everywhere except England? How did all of it get here?

The answer is a bit of a mix, and it is worth knowing the true version. Some of these treasures were bought, fair and square. Some were gifts. Some were dug up carefully by scientists who wanted to understand the past.

But some of them, and this is the honest part, were taken. Long ago, Britain was extremely powerful. Its ships sailed everywhere, and it ruled over huge parts of the world. When a country is that strong, it can carry off other people's treasures and bring them home, sometimes by asking, and sometimes by simply taking.

So today, some of the countries these treasures came from would like them back. They say, that belonged to us, our ancestors made it, please return it. And people argue about this a great deal, in serious meetings and in newspapers, even right now.

You will bump into this puzzle for real today. The famous Rosetta Stone was carried off after a war. The beautiful Greek carvings you may see were taken from a temple in Athens, and Greece has been politely asking for them back for two hundred years.

You do not have to decide who is right. Even grown ups cannot agree. But as you look at each amazing thing, you can quietly ask yourself the really interesting question, who made this, and who should get to keep it? Ask that, and you are not just a visitor. You are a proper museum detective.
