# What Was for Dinner in Jorvik?
You already know the famous poo tells us what one Viking ate. But scientists have studied seeds, bones, nutshells and rubbish pits from all over Jorvik, so we actually know a huge amount about Viking dinner. And some of it might surprise you.

First, the big rule. There were no potatoes, no tomatoes, no chocolate, no bananas, and no sugar as we know it. All of those come from faraway parts of the world that Europeans had not reached yet. A Viking child would never once have tasted a chip or a piece of chocolate. Imagine that.

So what did they eat? Lots of bread, dark and heavy, made from grain crushed between two big stones. Here is the not-so-tasty part. Little bits of grit from the grinding stones ended up in the flour, so every loaf was slightly gritty, and over a lifetime all that grit wore Viking teeth flat and smooth, like little pebbles. Scientists can often spot a Viking skull just from the worn-down teeth.

They ate meat when they could, beef, pork, mutton and chicken, plus eggs. They ate a lot of fish, both from the river and, more and more as the years went by, from the sea, herring and eel and more. They kept cows and goats for milk, and turned it into cheese and butter. From their gardens and the wild they got cabbages, leeks, onions, peas and beans, and they gathered apples, plums, cherries, blackberries and hazelnuts. For something sweet there was honey, the only sweet treat they had, and it was precious. To drink there was milk, water, and ale, a kind of weak beer, which even children sipped a little of, because it was cleaner than the dirty river water.

And nothing was wasted. Bones were boiled for soup and marrow, then sometimes carved into tools, pins, skates and toys. A Viking kitchen threw almost nothing away, which is lucky for us, because their leftovers and rubbish are exactly how we know all this.

So a Viking feast was hearty, brown and salty, full of bread and meat and fish and ale, sweetened only by honey and berries, and crunchy with just a little too much grit. Delicious, mostly. Hard on the teeth. And absolutely, definitely, no chocolate.
